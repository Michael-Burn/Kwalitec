These invariants specialise educational law at the Learning Episode grain. Where conflict appears with higher documents, higher law prevails until formal amendment aligns them.

---

## 1. Purpose

Learning Episode invariants are permanent constraints on how episodes are designed, selected, executed, evaluated, and narrated.

If a proposed tutoring feature would break an invariant, it is educationally unlawful until redesigned.

---

## 2. How to Read an Invariant

Each invariant has:

- **Statement** — binding rule  
- **Rationale** — why it exists  
- **Forbids** — characteristic violations  
- **Requires** — characteristic lawful behaviour  

Catalogue size: **twenty** episode invariants (E1–E20).

---

## 3. Episode Invariants

### E1 — Exactly one educational purpose

**Statement:** Every Learning Episode has exactly one deliberate educational purpose — one intentional educational improvement.

**Rationale:** Multiple purposes destroy diagnosability and evaluation honesty.

**Forbids:** Bundled goals (“introduce X and revise Y and exam-practice Z”); chapter-sized purposes.

**Requires:** A single named teaching goal per episode.

---

### E2 — Learning objective required

**Statement:** A Learning Episode cannot exist without a curriculum-grounded learning objective.

**Rationale:** Teaching without an aim is activity theatre.

**Forbids:** Episodes anchored only to “Chapter 4,” timers, or engagement hooks.

**Requires:** Explicit objective identity for every episode instance.

---

### E3 — Every episode produces evidence

**Statement:** Every Learning Episode must produce at least one material educational evidence item attributable to its purpose.

**Rationale:** Without evidence, tutoring cannot observe or adapt.

**Forbids:** Closed episodes with only “content shown” and no observation.

**Requires:** Evidence collection as a lifecycle obligation.

---

### E4 — Every episode updates understanding provisionally

**Statement:** Every completed episode must contribute to a provisional update of educational belief about the student’s understanding or related capability — including the lawful update “inconclusive / unchanged.”

**Rationale:** Episodes exist to change or inform educational state; silence is not honesty.

**Forbids:** Episodes that leave no interpretative trace; pretending delivery equals improvement.

**Requires:** Episode evaluation that can say advanced / partial / not / inconclusive.

**Note:** “Updates understanding” means updates the *educational reading* of understanding, not that understanding always improved.

---

### E5 — Episodes never declare mastery

**Statement:** No Learning Episode may declare mastery (or equivalent strong attainment) as its completion outcome.

**Rationale:** Mastery requires accumulated multi-dimensional evidence over time.

**Forbids:** “Mastered” badges on episode close; single-success mastery language.

**Requires:** Modest evaluation language; mastery remains Twin-estimated from accumulated evidence.

---

### E6 — Reflection belongs to the episode

**Statement:** Reflection is part of the episode’s educational close (or lawfully attributed to the episode within a session micro-sequence), not optional decoration detached from purpose.

**Rationale:** Metacognitive signal must attach to the work that produced it.

**Forbids:** Reflection-free tutoring episodes presented as complete; orphan reflections with no episode attribution.

**Requires:** Reflection stage with consequence for the next educational decision.

---

### E7 — Assessment without educational purpose is prohibited

**Statement:** Assessment elicitation inside or as an episode is lawful only when it serves a declared educational purpose (diagnosis, post-teach check, retrieval, calibration, etc.).

**Rationale:** Surveillance testing is not tutoring.

**Forbids:** Score-harvesting with no teaching goal; endless testing as a substitute for instruction when instruction is the diagnosed need.

**Requires:** Named purpose for every assessed episode.

---

### E8 — Teaching before post-instruction judgement of the taught aim

**Statement:** Within an episode whose purpose includes improving a diagnosed aim, instructional opportunity precedes assessment used to claim post-instruction competence on that aim.

**Rationale:** Aligns with educational invariant I3 at episode grain.

**Forbids:** High-stakes judgement of a newly targeted gap with no teach move.

**Requires:** Instruction stage (however brief) before competence claims; diagnostic probes remain lawful before teaching.

---

### E9 — Misconceptions must be resolved or recorded

**Statement:** When an episode encounters evidence of a misconception, the misconception must be explicitly addressed in-episode or explicitly recorded as unresolved for later Misconception Repair — never silently ignored.

**Rationale:** Practice on a wrong model strengthens the wrong model.

**Forbids:** Hoping volume will dissolve patterned conceptual error; deleting misconception flags without evidence of repair.

**Requires:** Disposition: repaired / reduced / persists / newly recorded.

---

### E10 — Strategy must match diagnosis

**Statement:** The episode’s teaching strategy and type must be justifiable from educational diagnosis (or lawful cold-start sequencing).

**Rationale:** Mismatched strategy wastes the student’s scarce time.

**Forbids:** Default “more questions” for every need; novelty-driven type selection.

**Requires:** Explainable diagnosis → strategy → type chain.

---

### E11 — Atomicity of capability improvement

**Statement:** Every Learning Episode should improve one educational capability (Educational Atomicity), not a syllabus region.

**Rationale:** See [`EDUCATIONAL_ATOMICITY.md`](EDUCATIONAL_ATOMICITY.md).

**Forbids:** “Teach all GLMs”; “revise the whole chapter” as one episode purpose.

**Requires:** Capability-scoped teaching goals.

---

### E12 — Dimensional aim required

**Statement:** Every episode must name at least one primary learning dimension it intends to advance (Understanding, Application, Connection, Retention, or Transfer).

**Rationale:** Aligns with Learning Model and educational invariant I1.

**Forbids:** Dimensionless busywork.

**Requires:** Explicit dimensional aim compatible with the single purpose.

---

### E13 — Completion is not learning

**Statement:** Completing an episode answers whether the engagement closed under its criteria; it does not by itself prove that learning occurred.

**Rationale:** Completion is administrative/educational process closure; learning is evidential capacity change.

**Forbids:** Narrating “episode complete” as “learned” or “understood.”

**Requires:** Separate narration of completion vs evaluation vs twin estimates.

---

### E14 — Evidence relevance

**Statement:** Evidence collected in an episode must be relevant to the episode’s declared purpose and learning objective.

**Rationale:** Irrelevant metrics create false evaluation confidence.

**Forbids:** Counting minutes, streaks, or off-objective clicks as purpose evidence.

**Requires:** Expected-evidence profile per episode type.

---

### E15 — No second purpose mid-flight

**Statement:** If educational need changes such that a second distinct purpose appears, the current episode must close (complete or abort) and a new episode begin.

**Rationale:** Protects E1 and evaluation integrity.

**Forbids:** Quietly expanding scope mid-episode to “also fix” unrelated deficits.

**Requires:** Explicit episode boundary when purpose changes.

---

### E16 — Abort preserves educational truth

**Statement:** An aborted episode must still record what occurred (evidence of attempt, failure mode, reflection if any) and feed the next educational decision.

**Rationale:** Erasing struggle destroys diagnosis.

**Forbids:** Silent discard of aborted work as if it never happened.

**Requires:** Abort outcomes as first-class educational events.

---

### E17 — Twin authority boundary

**Statement:** Episodes supply evidence and evaluation inputs to the Student Digital Twin; they do not own or overwrite Twin mastery/readiness belief as educational authority.

**Rationale:** Twin interprets accumulated evidence; episodes are producers of observations.

**Forbids:** Episode-local mastery writes; circular treatment of twin scores as new raw evidence without observation.

**Requires:** Clear producer/consumer boundary.

---

### E18 — Curriculum primacy of aims

**Statement:** Episode learning objectives remain accountable to official curriculum identity; episodes may not invent a private competing syllabus as truth.

**Rationale:** Professional examinations are syllabus-bound.

**Forbids:** Orphan aims with no curriculum grounding presented as core truth.

**Requires:** Objective identifiers (or equivalent lawful curriculum references).

---

### E19 — Explainable selection

**Statement:** Why this episode was selected now must be expressible in plain educational language to the student (or auditor).

**Rationale:** Trust and tutor obligation require justification.

**Forbids:** Opaque ranking outputs with no educational translation.

**Requires:** What / why / what follows for episode selection.

---

### E20 — UI and storage do not redefine episodes

**Statement:** Screens, components, activities, prompts, and database rows may project episodes but must not redefine educational episode identity or waive E1–E19 for implementation convenience.

**Rationale:** Architecture independence; prevents accidental ontology collapse.

**Forbids:** “One card = one episode” as educational law; “one row = mastery.”

**Requires:** Mapping discipline that preserves one-purpose educational identity.

---

## 4. Meta-Rules

### EM1 — Invariants outrank convenience

Implementation convenience, UI simplicity, or engagement pressure cannot waive E1–E20.

### EM2 — Higher law

Conflict with Educational Invariants (I1–I20), Educational Principles, or the Constitution is resolved upward until amendment.

### EM3 — Amendment discipline

Episode invariants change only by deliberate educational governance amendment.

### EM4 — Testability

Designs should be reviewable against each invariant with a clear educational pass/fail judgement.

## 6. Summary

These twenty invariants keep Learning Episodes as honest tutoring units: one purpose, one objective, real evidence, real reflection, no mastery theatre, and no implementation-driven redefinition of education.
