**Nature:** Documentation only — no runtime behaviour

## 1. Purpose

This document states **educational invariants**: rules that may never be violated in Kwalitec’s educational design, behaviour, or claims.

Invariants are not preferences, heuristics, or temporary product guidelines. They are permanent constraints. If a proposed feature, recommendation, message, or workflow would break an invariant, the proposal is educationally unlawful until redesigned — or until this document and any higher constitutional authority are formally amended.

These invariants derive from the Educational Domain Model, Learning Model, Understanding Model, Tutor Model, and the Educational Constitution. They specialise those doctrines into testable prohibitions and obligations.

Episode-grain specialisations (exactly one purpose, atomicity, UI independence, etc.) live in [`LEARNING_EPISODE_INVARIANTS.md`](LEARNING_EPISODE_INVARIANTS.md) and must be read together with this catalogue.

---

## 2. How to Read an Invariant

Each invariant has:

- **Statement** — the binding rule  
- **Rationale** — why the rule exists  
- **Forbids** — characteristic violations  
- **Requires** — characteristic lawful behaviour  

Approximate catalogue size: **twenty** core invariants (I1–I20), plus a short meta-rule set.

---

## 3. Core Educational Invariants

### I1 — Dimensional purpose of every learning episode

**Statement:** Every learning episode must be designed to improve at least one learning dimension (Understanding, Application, Connection, Retention, or Transfer).

**Rationale:** Episodes without dimensional aim are activity theatre.

**Forbids:** Closing empty “busywork” as educational progress; scheduling time with no learning objective.

**Requires:** An explicit educational aim for each episode.

---

### I2 — Completion never implies mastery

**Statement:** Completing a task, mission, session, episode, chapter, or plan never by itself implies mastery.

**Rationale:** Completion answers administrative closure; mastery requires durable, multi-source evidence.

**Forbids:** Student or system language that equates “done” with “mastered.”

**Requires:** Separate narration of coverage/completion and estimated mastery.

---

### I3 — Teaching precedes assessment of the taught aim

**Statement:** Within a coherent instructional cycle for a diagnosed need, teaching (or an equivalent instructional opportunity) precedes assessment of that same aim.

**Rationale:** Assessing what has not been taught on the diagnosed need is surveillance, not tutoring; it also confuses cold-start ignorance with failure after instruction.

**Forbids:** Defaulting to high-stakes judgement of a newly diagnosed gap with no instructional move.

**Requires:** A teach step (however brief) before claiming post-instruction performance on that aim.

**Note:** Diagnostic probes that *discover* need are lawful before teaching; they are not the same as declaring post-teaching competence.

---

### I4 — Reflection must influence future teaching

**Statement:** Student reflection captured after educational work must be available to, and capable of changing, subsequent educational diagnosis and teaching.

**Rationale:** Reflection without consequence is decorative.

**Forbids:** Collecting reflection that cannot affect the next tutor loop turn; fabricating reflection.

**Requires:** A path from reflection → interpretation → adaptation.

---

### I5 — Recommendations require educational justification

**Statement:** Every material recommendation of what to do next must include an educational justification grounded in evidence, objectives, or lawful curriculum sequencing — expressible in plain language.

**Rationale:** Unexplained steering destroys trust and invites opaque optimisation.

**Forbids:** “Because the ranking said so” with no educational translation; novelty-driven topic thrash.

**Requires:** Answers to what, why (educationally), and what follows.

---

### I6 — Misconceptions must be addressed explicitly

**Statement:** When evidence supports a misconception (a stable wrong model), teaching must address it explicitly rather than only assigning more undifferentiated practice.

**Rationale:** Practice on top of a wrong model strengthens the wrong model.

**Forbids:** Silent hope that volume will dissolve patterned conceptual error.

**Requires:** Contrast, correction, and re-check aimed at the misconception.

---

### I7 — Understanding is inferred from multiple evidence sources

**Statement:** Claims of understanding require converging evidence from more than one weak source; a single correct answer is never sufficient.

**Rationale:** Understanding is multi-indicator; false indicators abound.

**Forbids:** Promoting “understood” from one MCQ success or one self-report.

**Requires:** Modest language until multiple appropriate signals accumulate.

---

### I8 — Study coverage is not understanding

**Statement:** Records of what has been studied must not be presented or used as proof of understanding.

**Rationale:** Coverage and competence answer different questions.

**Forbids:** Dashboards or copy that treat studied topics as known topics.

**Requires:** Distinct educational states and language.

---

### I9 — Evidence precedes educational claim

**Statement:** Material claims about knowledge, understanding, competence, retention, transfer, or mastery must be supported by evidence; absence of evidence is not evidence of attainment.

**Rationale:** Constitutional honesty; cold start must not invent confidence.

**Forbids:** Confident mastery theatre on thin history.

**Requires:** Estimated/provisional framing where warrant is weak.

---

### I10 — Inference may not invent evidence

**Statement:** Educational inference may interpret observations; it may not fabricate observations or treat inferences as if they were raw evidence.

**Rationale:** Protects the evidence spine from circular self-validation.

**Forbids:** Writing synthetic “evidence” from a score alone presented as an independent observation.

**Requires:** Clear separation of observation and interpretation.

---

### I11 — Time spent is not learning

**Statement:** Duration of study activity must not be treated as sufficient evidence that learning occurred.

**Rationale:** Learning is dimensional capacity change, not clock time.

**Forbids:** Equating long sessions with educational success.

**Requires:** Outcome- and evidence-oriented evaluation of episodes.

---

### I12 — Confidence alone never certifies mastery

**Statement:** Self-reported confidence or felt readiness must not, by itself, raise mastery claims to certainty.

**Rationale:** Confidence is soft evidence and often miscalibrated in novices.

**Forbids:** “Student feels ready ⇒ mastered.”

**Requires:** Performance and retention/transfer evidence for strong claims.

---

### I13 — Retention claims require delayed evidence

**Statement:** Claims that knowledge is retained must be supported by evidence gathered after a meaningful interval, not only by same-episode success.

**Rationale:** Same-day peaks are not retention.

**Forbids:** Declaring lasting command from end-of-chapter performance alone.

**Requires:** Spaced or delayed probes where retention is asserted.

---

### I14 — Transfer claims require variation

**Statement:** Claims that a student can transfer knowledge must be supported by success under legitimate surface variation or novelty within syllabus scope.

**Rationale:** Template success is not transfer.

**Forbids:** Equating homework-clone scores with exam-flexible competence.

**Requires:** Varied stems, parameters, or formats before strong transfer language.

---

### I15 — The tutor loop may not skip interpretation

**Statement:** Adaptation of teaching or recommendations must follow interpretation of observed evidence; systems may not jump from raw events to opaque next actions without an interpretative educational step.

**Rationale:** Preserves Diagnose → Teach → Observe → Interpret → Adapt integrity.

**Forbids:** Whiplash topic changes from a single uninterpreted signal.

**Requires:** Documentable educational reading of what the evidence means.

---

### I16 — Curriculum primacy

**Statement:** Educational sequencing and aims remain accountable to official syllabus structure; tutoring must not invent a private competing syllabus as truth.

**Rationale:** Professional examinations are syllabus-bound.

**Forbids:** Orphan topics or aims with no syllabus grounding presented as core truth.

**Requires:** Objectives and journeys anchored in curriculum identity.

---

### I17 — Uncertainty must be namable

**Statement:** Where evidence is thin, conflicting, or stale, educational communication must express uncertainty rather than false precision.

**Rationale:** Trust collapses when confident language outruns warrant.

**Forbids:** Exact-sounding mastery labels without support.

**Requires:** Estimated, suggested, or provisional framing as appropriate.

---

### I18 — No silent erasure of educational history

**Statement:** Lawful educational history (evidence, progress posture, prior diagnoses of need) must not be silently destroyed when plans, containers, or contexts change.

**Rationale:** The learner’s history belongs to the learner; continuity is constitutional.

**Forbids:** Administrative resets that pretend the student never learned or erred.

**Requires:** Continuity-preserving migration or explicit, justified handling when history must change.

---

### I19 — Engagement metrics do not override learning truth

**Statement:** Streaks, points, notifications, or other engagement devices must not redefine learning success or override evidence-based educational decisions.

**Rationale:** Engagement can support habit; it is not an educational dimension.

**Forbids:** Recommending against educational need to protect a streak narrative.

**Requires:** Learning invariants outrank engagement optimisation.

---

### I20 — Mastery language is earned, provisional, and multi-dimensional

**Statement:** Mastery (or equivalent strong attainment language) may be used only as an evidence-backed estimate reflecting understanding, application, retention, and transfer over time — never as a one-shot badge.

**Rationale:** Mastery is the highest honesty bar in the domain model.

**Forbids:** Badge theatre; single-quiz mastery; completion-based mastery.

**Requires:** Accumulated multi-dimensional evidence and provisional framing.

---

## 4. Meta-Rules

### M1 — Invariants outrank convenience

Implementation convenience, interface simplicity, or marketing pressure cannot waive I1–I20.

### M2 — Higher law

If an invariant appears to conflict with the Educational Constitution, the Constitution prevails until formal amendment aligns them.

### M3 — Amendment discipline

Invariants change only by deliberate educational governance amendment — not by silent product drift.

### M4 — Testability

Future designs should be reviewable against each invariant with a clear pass/fail educational judgement.

## 6. Summary

These twenty invariants are the educational constitution’s operational edge for the Domain Model era. They exist so that Kwalitec cannot accidentally become a completion tracker, an engagement engine, or a question firehose while still calling itself a tutor.
