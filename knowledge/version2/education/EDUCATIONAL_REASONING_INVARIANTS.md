## 1. Purpose

This document states **Educational Reasoning Invariants**: permanent constraints on how Kwalitec may reason educationally before selecting and evaluating Learning Episodes.

These invariants specialise the Domain Educational Invariants and Tutor Model at the **reasoning grain**. Where conflict appears with higher constitutional law, higher law prevails until formal amendment aligns them.

Approximate catalogue size: **twenty** reasoning invariants (R1–R20), plus short meta-rules.

---

## 2. How to Read an Invariant

Each invariant has:

- **Statement** — the binding rule  
- **Rationale** — why the rule exists  
- **Forbids** — characteristic violations  
- **Requires** — characteristic lawful behaviour  

---

## 3. Core Reasoning Invariants

### R1 — Diagnosis precedes teaching

**Statement:** Targeted teaching of a diagnosed need may not begin without an Educational Diagnosis (including lawful cold-start diagnosis under curriculum sequencing).

**Rationale:** Teaching without diagnosis is broadcasting.

**Forbids:** Assigning undifferentiated content as if need were known when it was never named.

**Requires:** A named educational problem (or lawful introduction need) before intention and strategy.

---

### R2 — Teaching requires an educational intention

**Statement:** Every Learning Episode that claims to teach must be governed by a Teaching Intention stating the educational change sought.

**Rationale:** Episodes without intention are activity theatre.

**Forbids:** Calendar blocks, content titles, or UI flows presented as teaching without intended educational change.

**Requires:** An explicit, evaluable intention (expressed as an atomic Teaching Goal at episode grain).

---

### R3 — Intentions require diagnosis

**Statement:** A Teaching Intention may not be adopted without a supporting Educational Diagnosis (or lawful cold-start sequencing diagnosis).

**Rationale:** Intentions detached from need become opportunistic content pushing.

**Forbids:** “Let’s do transfer today” with no diagnosed transfer weakness or curriculum-justified challenge path.

**Requires:** Traceability from intention back to diagnosis.

---

### R4 — Hypotheses remain revisable

**Statement:** Educational Hypotheses may never be frozen against contradicting evidence; revision and replacement are obligatory when warrant changes.

**Rationale:** Dogmatic explanations produce wrong long teaching commitments.

**Forbids:** Continuing to teach as if a refuted hypothesis still governed.

**Requires:** Explicit demotion, revision, or replacement when evidence contradicts.

---

### R5 — Evidence outweighs assumptions

**Statement:** When assumptions about the learner conflict with available evidence, evidence governs diagnosis, hypothesis, and intention.

**Rationale:** Tutoring memory must not invent a preferred student.

**Forbids:** Preserving a flattering or convenient narrative against patterned observation.

**Requires:** Updating reasoning when observations disagree with prior belief.

---

### R6 — Confidence alone never determines diagnosis

**Statement:** Self-reported confidence or felt readiness may inform soft interpretation but may never alone determine Educational Diagnosis.

**Rationale:** Confidence is frequently miscalibrated; false and low confidence are themselves diagnostic categories requiring performance warrant.

**Forbids:** “Student feels ready ⇒ no problem” or “Student feels lost ⇒ conceptual misunderstanding” without performance evidence.

**Requires:** Convergence with observational evidence before strong diagnostic commitment.

---

### R7 — Recommendations require educational justification

**Statement:** Every material recommendation of what to do next must be justifiable by diagnosis and intention (and, where used, hypothesis and priority), expressible in educational language.

**Rationale:** Unexplained steering destroys trust and invites opaque optimisation.

**Forbids:** “Because the ranker said so” with no educational translation.

**Requires:** Answers to what, why educationally, and what follows.

---

### R8 — Every teaching intention targets one primary educational improvement

**Statement:** A Teaching Intention governing an episode must target exactly one primary educational improvement (Educational Atomicity).

**Rationale:** Bundled intentions make evidence uninterpretable and tutoring unaccountable.

**Forbids:** Co-equal multi-aim episodes (“repair misconception and finish the chapter and timed paper”).

**Requires:** One primary intention; secondary benefits remain non-governing.

---

### R9 — Teaching strategies serve intentions

**Statement:** A Teaching Strategy may be selected only as a means to a Teaching Intention; strategy may not redefine the educational aim.

**Rationale:** Methods exist for purposes; purposes do not exist for methods.

**Forbids:** Choosing interleaved practice because it is fashionable while the intention was misconception repair without contrast.

**Requires:** Explicit fit between strategy and intention/diagnosis class.

---

### R10 — Learning Episodes implement strategies

**Statement:** Learning Episodes are the bounded realisation of Teaching Strategy under Teaching Intention; episodes may not invent a separate educational purpose disconnected from the reasoning chain.

**Rationale:** Preserves unity of reasoning and enactment.

**Forbids:** Episode types selected for UI convenience against intention; “mystery episodes” with no reasoning parentage.

**Requires:** Traceability Episode ← Strategy ← Intention ← Diagnosis.

---

### R11 — Diagnosis does not select how to teach

**Statement:** Educational Diagnosis names the educational problem; it does not by itself constitute selection of Teaching Strategy or episode type.

**Rationale:** Separates *what is wrong* from *how to intervene*.

**Forbids:** Diagnosis statements that smuggle methods (“needs spaced retrieval”) without a distinct intention/strategy step.

**Requires:** Distinct reasoning stages for diagnosis, intention, and strategy.

---

### R12 — Hypotheses explain; they do not replace diagnosis

**Statement:** An Educational Hypothesis presupposes a diagnosis; explanatory belief may not be used as a substitute for naming the deficiency category and objective.

**Rationale:** “Because prerequisites” without stating the gap leaves teaching untargeted.

**Forbids:** Vague causal stories without a named problem.

**Requires:** Diagnosis first (analytically), hypothesis as explanation of that diagnosis.

---

### R13 — Teaching may test hypotheses

**Statement:** It is lawful — and often required — that Teaching Intention and Strategy be chosen to discriminate among competing hypotheses or to confirm a working hypothesis.

**Rationale:** Underdetermined evidence is common; undiscerning volume is wasteful and risky.

**Forbids:** Treating all teaching as mere content shipment with no explanatory stake.

**Requires:** Willingness to interpret episode outcomes as evidence about the hypothesis.

---

### R14 — Priority precedes intention when diagnoses compete

**Statement:** When multiple material diagnoses exist, Educational Priority must select the primary problem before a Teaching Intention is locked for the next episode.

**Rationale:** Without priority, tutors thrash or optimise for convenience.

**Forbids:** Random or engagement-driven selection among known competing needs.

**Requires:** Application of the Priority Model (or equivalent explicit educational ordering).

---

### R15 — Prerequisites outrank extension

**Statement:** A blocking prerequisite gap outranks extension into dependent material for the next governing intention.

**Rationale:** Priority Principle P1; prevents advanced theatre.

**Forbids:** Pushing dependent objectives while upstream blockers remain unaddressed.

**Requires:** Strengthen-prerequisite intentions when Gate 1 applies.

---

### R16 — Misconceptions outrank undifferentiated practice

**Statement:** An evidenced misconception on the active objective outranks undifferentiated practice volume on that objective.

**Rationale:** Priority Principle P2; Domain Invariant I6 specialised at reasoning grain.

**Forbids:** “More questions” as the response to patterned wrong models.

**Requires:** Repair-misconception intentions before practice-as-default.

---

### R17 — Twin updates follow evidence and reflection; they do not invent them

**Statement:** Student Digital Twin updates in the reasoning loop must be grounded in evidence (and lawful reflection); Twin estimates may not be fabricated to justify a preferred diagnosis or recommendation.

**Rationale:** Protects the evidence spine and Twin honesty.

**Forbids:** Writing Twin belief from narrative alone; treating Twin output as new raw evidence.

**Requires:** Clear separation of observation, interpretation, and standing estimate.

---

### R18 — Reflection must be able to revise reasoning

**Statement:** Captured reflection must be capable of changing subsequent diagnosis, hypothesis, intention, priority, or strategy.

**Rationale:** Reflection without consequence is decorative (Domain Invariant I4 at reasoning grain).

**Forbids:** Collecting reflection that cannot affect the next loop turn.

**Requires:** A path from reflection → interpretation → revised reasoning.

---

### R19 — Uncertainty must remain namable in reasoning

**Statement:** Where evidence is thin, conflicting, or stale, diagnosis and hypothesis must express uncertainty rather than false precision.

**Rationale:** Trust and educational safety collapse when confident reasoning outruns warrant.

**Forbids:** Exact-sounding deficiency labels without support; irrevisable “known” causes.

**Requires:** Tentative/working/strong confidence postures and provisional language as appropriate.

---

### R20 — Long-term learning outranks short-term progress theatre

**Statement:** Reasoning may not select intentions that knowingly sacrifice durable understanding, retention, or transfer for immediate completion, streaks, or same-day score optics.

**Rationale:** Priority Principle P5; product promise is learning.

**Forbids:** Coverage celebration that creates false confidence; skipping repair to protect a plan narrative.

**Requires:** Prefer durable educational goods when trade-offs appear.

## 4. Meta-Rules

### M1 — Reasoning invariants outrank convenience

Interface simplicity, implementation convenience, or marketing pressure cannot waive R1–R20.

### M2 — Higher law

If a reasoning invariant appears to conflict with the Educational Constitution or Domain Educational Invariants (I1–I20), higher law prevails until formal amendment aligns them.

### M3 — Amendment discipline

Reasoning invariants change only by deliberate educational governance amendment — not by silent product drift.

### M4 — Coherence with episode invariants

Learning Episode Invariants (E-series) constrain episode enactment; Reasoning Invariants constrain the thought that selects episodes. Both bind.

### M5 — Testability

Future designs should be reviewable against each reasoning invariant with a clear pass/fail educational judgement — without requiring a particular implementation.

## 6. Summary

These twenty invariants keep Kwalitec’s educational reasoning honest: diagnose before teaching, intend before strategising, keep hypotheses revisable, let evidence rule assumptions, and refuse progress theatre when durable learning is at stake.

They are the permanent reasoning constitution for Version 2 tutoring.
