## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **instructional principles** that govern Teaching Strategy selection and composition in Kwalitec Version 2.

These principles are educational constraints on *how* the tutor teaches once Teaching Intention is known. They complement — and do not replace — journey/session principles in [`../EDUCATIONAL_PRINCIPLES.md`](../EDUCATIONAL_PRINCIPLES.md) or the Educational Constitution.

Principles are qualitative governance. They are not algorithms, scoring functions, or UI rules.

---

## 2. Status of Principles

| Property | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Normative** | They bind strategy selection unless a documented educational exception applies |
| **Defeasible** | A principle may yield to a stronger diagnosis-specific duty (for example temporary re-scaffolding after a collapse) |
| **Non-substitutive** | Principles do not replace diagnosis, hypothesis, or intention |
| **Explainable** | Any material strategy choice should be narrable in principle language |

## 3. Principles

For each principle: Definition · Educational rationale · Tutor implications · Potential misuse.

---

### P1 — Understanding before memorisation

**Definition**  
Prefer establishing meaningful grasp of the target idea or method before demanding rote retention of forms, steps, or slogans.

**Educational rationale**  
Memorised fragments without understanding produce fragile performance, false confidence, and transfer failure. Retention strategies are powerful after a coherent structure exists to retain.

**Tutor implications**  
When diagnosis shows conceptual emptiness or misconception, prefer Direct Explanation, Analogy, Guided Discovery, Misconception Confrontation, or Dual Representation before Retrieval First / Spaced Reinforcement as primary moves. Use retrieval to consolidate understanding once a floor exists.

**Potential misuse**  
Blocking all memory work indefinitely “until perfect understanding”; refusing lawful retrieval of well-taught material near exams; treating every fluency need as a conceptual crisis.

---

### P2 — Concrete before abstract

**Definition**  
Move from tangible cases, numbers, tables, or familiar situations toward formal generalisation when the student lacks abstract footholds.

**Educational rationale**  
Abstraction without concrete anchors invites symbol manipulation without meaning. Professional actuarial abstraction is a destination, not a cold start for every learner state.

**Tutor implications**  
Prefer worked numerical cases, dual representation (concrete ↔ symbolic), and analogy before purely axiomatic presentation when understanding is thin. Fade toward abstract formulation as evidence of grasp appears.

**Potential misuse**  
Never ascending to required formal abstraction; trapping advanced students in unnecessary concrete theatre; confusing “concrete” with entertainment vignettes unrelated to the objective.

---

### P3 — Known before unknown

**Definition**  
Anchor new teaching in what the student already knows, can retrieve, or can partially explain — then extend into the unknown.

**Educational rationale**  
Learning is constructive. Ignoring prior knowledge wastes encoding opportunities and increases load. Ignoring *wrong* prior knowledge leaves misconceptions intact.

**Tutor implications**  
Activate prior knowledge (Retrieval First when lawful); use Analogy from known domains; repair wrong “knowns” via confrontation rather than layering novelty on error.

**Potential misuse**  
Endless activation without teaching; assuming “known” from syllabus position rather than evidence; using familiar wrong models as anchors without marking them unsafe.

---

### P4 — Simple before complex

**Definition**  
Sequence instructional demand from simpler cases and fewer interacting factors toward more complex, multi-condition tasks.

**Educational rationale**  
Complexity multiplies cognitive load and confounds evidence: failure on a complex item may not locate the weak facet. Atomic teaching requires controllable complexity.

**Tutor implications**  
Within Progressive Scaffolding and Deliberate Practice, begin with stripped cases; add conditions deliberately. Do not open Exam Simulation on a capability that has never succeeded in simple form.

**Potential misuse**  
Oversimplification that never meets syllabus-realistic complexity; permanent easy-item loops that avoid transfer; confusing “simple” with “trivial busywork.”

---

### P5 — Repair before extension

**Definition**  
When a stable misconception, critical prerequisite gap, or contradictory model blocks progress, repair or restore before extending to new related content.

**Educational rationale**  
Extension on a broken foundation amplifies error, wastes practice, and corrupts twin updates. Educational ethics require explicit repair.

**Tutor implications**  
Priority Model and diagnosis may elevate Repair misconception or Strengthen prerequisite over Build intuition on advanced material. Prefer Misconception Confrontation, Counterexample, and Error-Led Teaching before Interleaving or Exam Simulation on the contaminated region.

**Potential misuse**  
Infinite repair delay of all forward motion for minor slips; treating every error as misconception; refusing exam-horizon mixed practice when repair of a *narrow* facet can proceed in parallel under atomic episodes.

---

### P6 — Examples before independent performance

**Definition**  
Provide modelled or guided exemplars of legitimate performance before requiring full independence on a new method class.

**Educational rationale**  
Novices lack schemas for self-directed practice. Worked examples and think-aloud modelling reduce floundering and form correct procedures.

**Tutor implications**  
Default composition often includes Worked Example or Think-Aloud Modelling → Progressive Scaffolding → Faded Guidance → Independent Practice. Skip modelling only when evidence shows modelling would reverse expertise (student already fluent).

**Potential misuse**  
Endless examples without fade; copying blindness without prediction/completion demands; modelling when the real need is misconception confrontation.

---

### P7 — Guidance fades over time

**Definition**  
As understanding and accurate performance increase, instructional supports should decrease — unless new evidence warrants temporary re-scaffolding.

**Educational rationale**  
Permanent scaffolding produces dependent performance that collapses under exam and transfer conditions. Fading operationalises growth toward competence.

**Tutor implications**  
Prefer Faded Guidance as an explicit composition commitment after Guided Practice success. Treat rising hint dependence as a signal to revise strategy or diagnosis, not to add permanent crutches.

**Potential misuse**  
Fading on a schedule regardless of rising errors; abrupt abandonment labelled as “independence”; refusing temporary re-scaffold after legitimate setback.

---

### P8 — Retrieval strengthens memory

**Definition**  
Actively recalling target knowledge strengthens retention more reliably than restudy alone, provided a fair instructional foundation exists.

**Educational rationale**  
Retrieval practice is a durable learning mechanism. Teaching that never demands retrieval risks illusion of knowing.

**Tutor implications**  
Incorporate Retrieval First or Retrieval After Instruction when intention includes retention or consolidation. Pair with Spaced Reinforcement across sessions. Do not treat recognition clicks as retrieval.

**Potential misuse**  
Retrieval without prior teaching (cold-start cruelty or empty failure loops); retrieval that rehearses misconceptions; equating immediate echo with retrieval.

---

### P9 — Spacing improves durability

**Definition**  
Distributing revisits to taught material over time improves long-term retention relative to massed cramming of the same content.

**Educational rationale**  
Forgetting curves and spacing effects are central to exam-horizon honesty. Massed mastery theatre decays.

**Tutor implications**  
Prefer Spaced Reinforcement for Improve retention and exam preparation retention components. Preserve educational memory of what was taught so spacing is targeted, not random reopening of chapters.

**Potential misuse**  
Labelling cramming as spacing; spacing never-taught material; spacing so aggressive that re-teaching costs exceed benefit; using spacing to avoid repairing live misconceptions.

---

### P10 — Interleaving improves discrimination

**Definition**  
Mixing related problem types or concepts during practice improves the student’s ability to select which knowledge applies, compared with blocked practice of a single type.

**Educational rationale**  
Professional examinations and real tasks interleave. Blocked success often masks selection failure.

**Tutor implications**  
After single-node competence floors exist, prefer Interleaving for Improve transfer, Connect fragmented knowledge, and Prepare for examination. Interpret short-term performance dips cautiously — they are not automatic failure of the intention.

**Potential misuse**  
Interleaving at first exposure; mixing unresolved misconceptions; overload via too many categories; using interleaving as coverage theatre without a discrimination aim.

---

### P11 — Reflection consolidates learning

**Definition**  
Structured student reflection after instructional work helps consolidate understanding, surface mismatches between confidence and performance, and inform the next educational move.

**Educational rationale**  
Without reflection with consequence, episodes close administratively. Metacognition supports transfer, calibration, and honest twin updates.

**Tutor implications**  
Close strategy-governed episodes with reflection that can alter next strategy or intention. Treat reflection as soft evidence — influential, not sovereign over performance evidence.

**Potential misuse**  
Empty reflection forms; reflection without consequence; letting reflection override strong performance patterns or excuse missing repair; reflection as substitute for teaching.

---

### P12 — Transfer requires varied contexts

**Definition**  
Claims of transfer require successful application under legitimate surface variation, novelty, or rewording — not success on clones alone.

**Educational rationale**  
Clone fluency is not transfer. Version 2 educational honesty forbids transfer language from identical stems.

**Tutor implications**  
When intention is Improve transfer, select Interleaving, Concept Comparison, Dual Representation, Guided Discovery with variation, or Exam Simulation — and demand varied probes. Do not declare transfer from same-day identical items.

**Potential misuse**  
Variation so extreme it leaves syllabus scope; transfer theatre while misconception remains primary; endless variation before any stable core exists.

## 4. Principle Interactions

Principles often combine:

| Situation | Principle tension / resolution |
|-----------|--------------------------------|
| Exam soon, misconception live | **P5 Repair before extension** outranks early **Exam Simulation**; limited exam technique work may proceed on *uncontaminated* objectives |
| Fluent student, weak retention | **P8/P9** outrank further **P6** modelling |
| Novice, abstract topic | **P2** and **P6** before **P10** interleaving |
| Rising independence | **P7** fading; temporary return to scaffolds if evidence warrants (defeasible, not contradiction) |
| Strong performance, false confidence | Reflection (**P11**) + discriminating variation (**P12**) before more blocked practice |

When principles appear to conflict, resolve by: (1) diagnosis priority, (2) Educational Atomicity, (3) evidence strength, (4) exam-horizon honesty without conceptual abandonment.

---

## 5. Relationship to Strategy Selection

Instructional principles **filter and order** candidate strategies; they do not pick a unique strategy mechanically.

Selection Model rule of thumb:

1. Satisfy non-negotiable duties (repair, prerequisites, atomicity).  
2. Apply sequencing principles (understanding → memory; examples → independence; simple → complex).  
3. Apply durability and discrimination principles when intention and readiness warrant.  
4. Close with reflection and evidence-honest evaluation.

---

## 6. Summary Propositions

1. Instructional principles govern *how* strategies are chosen and composed.  
2. Principles are normative, defeasible, explainable, and non-substitutive for diagnosis.  
3. Understanding, concreteness, known anchors, simplicity, and repair precede extension, independence, and advanced durability tactics when foundations are weak.  
4. Retrieval, spacing, interleaving, reflection, and varied contexts govern consolidation and transfer once foundations exist.  
5. Misuse typically arises from treating a principle as absolute fashion rather than diagnosis-relative duty.
