## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **educational categories** of Learning Episodes.

Episode types answer: *what kind of single educational improvement is this episode for?* They are pedagogical categories, not UI templates and not content files.

Types may be realised by many different materials. Materials do not invent new educational types by accident of format.

---

## 2. Catalogue Principles

1. **One type, one primary purpose** — types encode educational intent.  
2. **Selection follows diagnosis** — type is chosen because it fits need, not because it fills a calendar.  
3. **Evidence must match type** — expected evidence is part of the type definition.  
4. **Duration is typical, not law** — bands guide design; atomicity outranks filling time.  
5. **Prerequisites are educational** — unmet prerequisites should trigger Prerequisite Repair (or equivalent) rather than silent failure theatre.

**Typical duration bands (illustrative):**

| Band | Approximate span |
|------|------------------|
| Short | 5–15 minutes |
| Medium | 15–35 minutes |
| Long | 35–60 minutes |

Professional actuarial topics often need medium bands; recovery and reflection may be short.

## 3. Episode Type Definitions

### 3.1 Concept Introduction

**Educational objective**  
Establish first meaningful grasp of a concept: recognition plus initial explanation of meaning and conditions of use.

**When it should be selected**  
Cold start on a learning objective; diagnosis shows absence rather than misconception; curriculum sequencing places the concept as newly due.

**Expected evidence**  
Own-words paraphrase; identification of the concept in simple probes; statement of a basic condition of validity.

**Completion criteria**  
Student can recognise and give a minimal correct explanation sufficient to enter deepening or guided practice — not mastery.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Curriculum prerequisites for the objective satisfied, or concurrently scheduled Prerequisite Repair for critical gaps only.

---

### 3.2 Concept Deepening

**Educational objective**  
Strengthen conceptual understanding beyond first exposure: relationships, boundary conditions, and common traps.

**When it should be selected**  
Introduction has occurred; explanation is thin, fragile, or incomplete; application errors suggest conceptual shallowness rather than pure fluency deficit.

**Expected evidence**  
Richer explanation; contrast with near concepts; correct rejection of invalid applications; answers to “why” / “what if.”

**Completion criteria**  
Evidence of improved explanatory depth on the single targeted concept facet — not full topic coverage.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Prior Concept Introduction (or equivalent evidence of recognition/explanation floor).

---

### 3.3 Concept Connection

**Educational objective**  
Link the target idea to related syllabus ideas — shared structure across topics or within a topic.

**When it should be selected**  
Diagnosis shows pigeonhole knowledge; upcoming combination demands; student can apply locally but fails when ideas must relate.

**Expected evidence**  
Explicit mapping statements; correct use of a prior idea inside a later task; recognition of shared structure across two presentations.

**Completion criteria**  
Demonstrated relational link for the declared connection aim.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Basic grasp of each node being connected (introduction-level or better).

---

### 3.4 Worked Example

**Educational objective**  
Make a legitimate method intelligible by studying a complete, annotated solution path before independent demand.

**When it should be selected**  
New procedure; high cognitive load expected; diagnosis indicates need for modelling before practice; fading toward guided practice planned next.

**Expected evidence**  
Student can explain steps in the example; identify why a step is taken; predict the next step in a paused example (completion problems).

**Completion criteria**  
Student can narrate the method’s structure from the example without mere copying blindness.

**Typical duration**  
Short to medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Concept Introduction (or concurrent minimal concept cue) for symbols and meaning.

---

### 3.5 Guided Practice

**Educational objective**  
Practise application with scaffolds that are deliberately present and planned to fade.

**When it should be selected**  
After worked example or teaching; student not yet ready for full independence; errors still informative under support.

**Expected evidence**  
Correct or improving solutions with scaffold use; decreasing need for hints; partial justifications.

**Completion criteria**  
Reliable performance under reduced scaffolding on the targeted method family — readiness signal for Independent Practice, not mastery.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Instructional exposure to the method (Worked Example / Concept Introduction as appropriate).

---

### 3.6 Independent Practice

**Educational objective**  
Strengthen application and procedural fluency without scaffolds on a narrowly defined task family.

**When it should be selected**  
Guided practice success; diagnosis names fluency/application as the bottleneck; retention not yet the primary aim.

**Expected evidence**  
Accurate independent solutions; appropriate method selection within the family; reasonable efficiency.

**Completion criteria**  
Stable success on the declared practice aim under independence — still not transfer or mastery by itself.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Guided Practice (or strong equivalent evidence of readiness).

---

### 3.7 Misconception Repair

**Educational objective**  
Replace one stable wrong mental model with a correct one through explicit contrast and re-check.

**When it should be selected**  
Patterned errors support a named misconception; undifferentiated practice would strengthen the wrong model.

**Expected evidence**  
Correct discrimination tasks; explanation of the corrected model; success on probes that previously triggered the misconception; reflection acknowledging the former confusion.

**Completion criteria**  
Misconception marked reduced or resolved *for this probe set*, or explicitly recorded as persisting — never silently ignored.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Sufficient concept vocabulary to understand the contrast; otherwise Concept Introduction first.

---

### 3.8 Retrieval Practice

**Educational objective**  
Strengthen durable access to knowledge or procedures via active recall with feedback.

**When it should be selected**  
Prior learning exists; diagnosis or schedule indicates retention risk; spacing window due; not a substitute for first teaching.

**Expected evidence**  
Successful recall/performance after cue; error patterns; time-to-retrieve signals where lawful.

**Completion criteria**  
Retrieval attempts completed with feedback on the targeted items; retention claim remains provisional until delayed success accumulates.

**Typical duration**  
Short to medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Prior instructional episode on the same objective.

---

### 3.9 Revision

**Educational objective**  
Re-engage previously studied material to restore or confirm capacity — within a single objective or tightly bounded objective set treated as one revision aim.

**When it should be selected**  
Journey revision intent; Adaptive Decision / tutor diagnosis marks material as due; exam proximity with lawful revision mode.

**Expected evidence**  
Performance on revisited objective; comparison with prior episode outcomes; reflection on felt forgetting vs actual performance.

**Completion criteria**  
Revision aim checked; outcome feeds twin/next decision — revision complete ≠ topic mastered.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Prior first-pass learning history for the objective (continuity preserved).

**Atomicity note:** “Revise Chapter 4” is not an episode. Revision episodes name one deliberate revision aim.

---

### 3.10 Exam Application

**Educational objective**  
Practise syllabus-valid performance under exam-like constraints (wording, time pressure, mark-style demand) for one targeted objective or tightly scoped combination treated as one aim.

**When it should be selected**  
Application/transfer readiness emerging; mock or exam-prep phase; diagnosis needs exam-format evidence.

**Expected evidence**  
Exam-format attempt quality; method selection under constraint; partial-credit patterns where modelled.

**Completion criteria**  
Exam-format probe completed and evaluated against the teaching goal; not a certification of exam readiness alone.

**Typical duration**  
Medium to long.

**Prerequisites**  
Independent Practice and/or Transfer Practice readiness; concept floor established.

---

### 3.11 Error Analysis

**Educational objective**  
Build analytical understanding by diagnosing flaws in incorrect or incomplete solutions related to one objective.

**When it should be selected**  
Student produces answers but cannot critique; recurring slips hide principle errors; deepening analysis-level understanding.

**Expected evidence**  
Correct identification of the flaw; principle-based explanation of why it fails; corrected approach.

**Completion criteria**  
Successful critique on the targeted error class.

**Typical duration**  
Short to medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Enough grasp to understand a correct approach; otherwise teach first.

---

### 3.12 Reflection

**Educational objective**  
Strengthen metacognitive accuracy: naming what is known, unknown, difficult, and uncertain for a specific objective — as an episode whose *purpose* is reflection quality (distinct from the reflection *stage* that closes other episodes).

**When it should be selected**  
Calibration problems; after dense practice with thin metacognition; when tutor needs richer soft evidence before strategy change.

**Expected evidence**  
Specific, non-generic reflection; alignment or misalignment with performance evidence.

**Completion criteria**  
Structured reflection captured with consequence for next decision.

**Typical duration**  
Short.

**Prerequisites**  
Recent educational work to reflect upon.

**Note:** Ordinary episodes still include a reflection stage. This type is used when reflection itself is the deliberate educational improvement.

---

### 3.13 Synthesis

**Educational objective**  
Integrate multiple facets of one objective (or a tightly bound pair already connected) into a coherent explanatory or solution whole.

**When it should be selected**  
Parts exist in isolation; student needs a unifying structure; preparation for capstone or exam combination within atomic scope.

**Expected evidence**  
Coherent multi-step explanation or solution that uses the integrated parts intentionally.

**Completion criteria**  
Demonstrated synthesis of the declared integration aim.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Component episodes completed at introduction/practice floor.

---

### 3.14 Confidence Calibration

**Educational objective**  
Improve alignment between felt confidence and evidenced performance on one objective.

**When it should be selected**  
Systematic over- or under-confidence; soft evidence diverges from performance; pre-exam honesty work.

**Expected evidence**  
Pre-task confidence judgements vs outcomes; post-task recalibration reflection.

**Completion criteria**  
Calibration exercise completed; residual miscalibration recorded honestly.

**Typical duration**  
Short to medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Performance tasks available on the same objective.

---

### 3.15 Recovery

**Educational objective**  
Restore productive learning conditions after overload, frustration, repeated failure, or session breakdown — returning the student to a diagnosable, teachable state for a specific objective.

**When it should be selected**  
Affective/cognitive overload; abort of a prior episode; error cascade; tutor judges continued demand would harm learning.

**Expected evidence**  
Stabilised engagement; clarified next micro-goal; reduced panic indicators in reflection; successful tiny probe if used.

**Completion criteria**  
Student ready for a normal episode type again; failure recorded without shame theatre.

**Typical duration**  
Short.

**Prerequisites**  
None educational beyond an identified stuck state; may temporarily suspend hard prerequisites.

---

### 3.16 Prerequisite Repair

**Educational objective**  
Repair one missing or weak prerequisite that blocks the current objective — atomic repair, not wholesale earlier-chapter revision.

**When it should be selected**  
Diagnosis shows the current failure is prerequisite-caused; curriculum graph marks a dependency; patterned collapse on an earlier idea.

**Expected evidence**  
Restored performance on the prerequisite aim; successful return probe on the blocked objective (optional same-session or next episode).

**Completion criteria**  
Prerequisite aim advanced enough to unblock; residual gap recorded if not.

**Typical duration**  
Short to medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Identification of the specific prerequisite objective.

---

### 3.17 Transfer Practice

**Educational objective**  
Strengthen transfer: apply knowledge under legitimate surface variation within syllabus scope for one objective.

**When it should be selected**  
Independent practice success on clones; exam novelty expected; understanding inference needs variation evidence.

**Expected evidence**  
Success on reworded/reparametrised/restructured tasks; justification of method under change.

**Completion criteria**  
Demonstrated transfer on the declared variation class — still not full mastery.

**Typical duration**  
Medium.

**Prerequisites**  
Independent Practice (or equivalent) on the base form.

---

### 3.18 Capstone

**Educational objective**  
Demonstrate integrated, exam-credible performance on a single capstone aim for a topic arc — still one purpose, still not automatic mastery certification.

**When it should be selected**  
Journey nearing completion criteria; synthesis and transfer floors met; need for converging evidence on the topic’s governing objective set collapsed into one capstone aim.

**Expected evidence**  
Extended performance; explanation; handling of mixed demand within the declared capstone scope.

**Completion criteria**  
Capstone attempt evaluated; contributes strongly to journey completion *inputs* — does not by itself write Topic Complete or Mastery.

**Typical duration**  
Long.

**Prerequisites**  
Introduction, practice, and at least some transfer/revision evidence on constituent aims.

**Atomicity note:** Capstone aims must still be one deliberate educational purpose (for example “value a standard assurance–annuity package under stated bases”), not “do the whole syllabus.”

## 4. Selection Guidance (Summary)

| If diagnosis shows… | Prefer |
|---------------------|--------|
| Never seen / no recognition | Concept Introduction |
| Thin explanation | Concept Deepening |
| Isolated knowledge | Concept Connection |
| New procedure, high load | Worked Example → Guided Practice |
| Needs fluency | Independent Practice |
| Stable wrong model | Misconception Repair |
| Forgetting risk | Retrieval Practice / Revision |
| Exam-format need | Exam Application / Transfer Practice |
| Cannot critique errors | Error Analysis |
| Metacognitive fog | Reflection / Confidence Calibration |
| Overload | Recovery |
| Blocked by earlier idea | Prerequisite Repair |
| Parts not integrated | Synthesis |
| Journey completion evidence needed | Capstone |

---

## 5. Governance

1. New episode types require amendment of this catalogue with objective, selection rule, evidence, completion, duration, and prerequisites.  
2. Product features must map to types; they must not invent silent parallel categories.  
3. Types remain educational; UI may group or rename for students if meaning is preserved.

---

## 6. Summary Propositions

1. Eighteen episode types cover the primary tutoring intents of Kwalitec.  
2. Selection is diagnosis-driven.  
3. Each type defines evidence and completion without conferring mastery.  
4. Atomicity constrains Revision, Capstone, and similar broad-sounding types.
